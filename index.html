FILE/ 002 — RESEARCH PROGRAM FOUR AREAS · ONE LAB

Research

We build small machines that do big things — micromixers, microgrippers, untethered magnetic microrobots, and flow-visualization systems that turn biology into quantifiable fluid dynamics.

AREA 01 / BioMEMS

Biomedical micro-electro-mechanical systems

生醫微機電

BioMEMS are at the heart of everything we do. From serpentine microchannels that capture and orient zebrafish larvae without harming them, to artificial cilia arrays that activate sperm motility, our devices turn the microscale into a programmable experimental stage.

A recent line of work uses shape memory alloy (SMA) actuation to build miniaturized biomaterial-printing nozzles and catheter-intervention actuators — devices small enough to enter the vasculature yet robust enough for real biomedical tasks.

KEY PAPERS
  • — Huang et al., Sensors & Actuators B, 2017
  • — Mani et al., Sci. Reports, 2016
  • — Lu et al., Cardiovasc. Eng. Tech., 2018
  • — Mani et al., Biochip J., 2020
AREA 02 / MICROFABRICATION

Artificial cilia & magnetic microrobots

微系統製造技術

The lab has spent over a decade refining magnetically-actuated artificial cilia — PDMS-based hair-like structures that beat, bundle, and school like biological cilia. By distributing them in fish-schooling patterns or driving them asymmetrically, we induce 3-D vortical flow inside 2-D microchannels for high-performance mixing and propulsion.

More recently, we've scaled up to untethered microrobots and microgrippers — millimeter-scale agents controlled by external magnetic fields, with building-block modularity and multi-robot choreography for flow manipulation.

KEY PAPERS
  • — Chen et al., Lab on a Chip, 2013
  • — Chen et al., Chem. Eng. J., 2015 COVER
  • — Loganathan et al., Adv. Mater. Tech., 2022
  • — Loganathan et al., Adv. Intell. Syst., 2024
AREA 03 / HEMODYNAMICS

Cardiovascular flow in disease & development

心血管疾病血液動力學分析

On the clinical side, the lab analyzes patient-specific hemodynamics — abdominal aortic aneurysms, pulmonary arteries, stent-graft endoleaks — using a combination of CFD modeling and particle image velocimetry. We also study zebrafish cardiovascular function as a high-throughput surrogate for drug and additive screening.

A long-running collaboration with Prof. Kerem Pekkan (CMU → Koç University) applies these tools to embryonic great-vessel microcirculation and neonatal cardiopulmonary bypass.

KEY PAPERS
  • — Chen et al., J. Biomech. Eng. (ASME), 2014
  • — Lu et al., Comp. Math. Meth. Med., 2016
  • — Bordones et al., Ann. Biomed. Eng., 2018
  • — Subendran et al., Sci. Reports, 2021
AREA 04 / FLOW VISUALIZATION

Seeing what's too small to see

流場可視化

We develop and apply flow-visualization methods — confocal μPIV, OCT-μPIV, stereoscopic PIV — to resolve pulsatile, three-dimensional flow fields inside microchannels and living embryos. The lab's OCT-μPIV technique, developed at Carnegie Mellon, enables non-invasive depth-resolved flow measurement in tissue.

Visualization also serves our industrial collaborations — notably wafer-rinse hydrodynamics at the NCKU-TSMC Joint Developed Project, where understanding flow at the microscale directly translates into process yield.

KEY PAPERS
  • — Chen et al., Experiments in Fluids, 2013 (OCT-μPIV)
  • — Chen et al., Biorheology, 2011
  • — Chen et al., J. Fluids Eng. (ASME), 2018
  • — Pekkan et al., Experiments in Fluids, 2016
